The Career of Robin Williams

"I left school and couldn't find acting work, so I started going to clubs where you could do stand-up. I've always improvised, and stand-up was this great release. All of a sudden, it was just me and the audience." - Robin Williams

Where did Williams Get His Start?

  • Williams got his start doing standup comedy in the San Francisco Bay Area in the mid 1970s.
  • After moving to Los Angeles and continuing standup, Williams was approached by George Schlatter, a producer who helped him get his start in Television.
  • After his start in television, Williams went on to feature in the tremendously popular series Mork and Mindy, a hugely popular spinoff of the series Happy Days.
  • In 1977, Williams gained his first film credit, but did not star in a leading role until the 1980 film Popeye.
  • After spending time in film and television, as well as continuing his stand-up comedy career, Robin Williams starred in Good Morning, Vietnam, in which his script was almost entirely improvised. This role is what truly brough him into the spotlight.

The Many Talents of Robin Williams

Through his career, Robin Williams proved his skill in various fields of performing arts:

  • Stand-Up Comedy
  • Improv
  • Acting
  • Live Theatre

Throughout his career in the arts, Williams also displayed a strong ability to play a wide variety of characters, from the animated Genie in Aladdin, to a female housekeeper in Mrs. Doubtfire.

Common Collaborators of Robin Williams

Williams spent ten years working with Pam Dawber during their time as co-stars on the hit show Mork and Mindy.

After his entrance into the world of film, Williams worked on five films, including Aladdin and Jumanji, with Frank Welker, a fellow voice actor.
