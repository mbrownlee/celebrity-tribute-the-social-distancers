Robin Williams: Newsworthy

Posthumous YouTube Channel Release

On April 1st, YouTube launched an official Robin Williams channel featuring some classic stand-up. His estate is responsible for setting up the channel as a way to "celebrate the life and comic genius” that is the late Williams, according to Tom Skinner of NME. More content will continue to be added.

Art Collection Auction

This item of news isn't the most in-depth to report, however it is cute. On April 13th, we learned via Jon Kawamoto of Bay Area News Group, that some pieces from Robin Williams' art collection will be up for auction. If you're a fan of the 2006 film, "Night At the Museum," you'll recognize this taxidermy piece as Crystal the monkey. No word on the benefactor of the auction.

Robin's Fashion "Drip"

The shelter-in-place order has Zelda Williams, Robin's daughter, going through old momentos this past week. She is quoted on Twitter saying, “Dad casually, daily wore things I have never seen another person dare wear, and I have always respected that flex.” W Magazine even picked up on her tweets and presented a few pictures intending to prove Robin as “legit a style god” and “the drip lord,” as claimed by twitter fans.
